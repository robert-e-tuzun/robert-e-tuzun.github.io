About me

Hi. My name is Robert E. Tuzun, and I write scientific/mathematical software and develop scientific/mathematical algorithms. Currently, I am a Scientist at the Naval Surface Warfare Center, Carderock Division in Maryland, working in a Scrum-like environment to fix bugs and add features to a mostly C++ multi-million line suite of naval applications. In addition to programming for more than the past 20 years, I have taught numerical methods, simulation and modeling, and scientific programming at the college level.

My strongest language is C++11/14/17 (with Boost), in desktop and massively parallel environments, but I have also programmed in Java, Python, Matlab, Fortran, and other languages. My software development of some quite complex applications has resulted in 35 peer-reviewed publications and two Ph.D.'s (chemistry and mathematics). During my academic career, it also strongly contributed to my achievement of early promotion and tenure, by two years.

On the side, to broaden my skills, I am studying topics in data science (data cleaning; languages and tools; and visualization and presentation). Also, to retain my high performance computing skills, I am continuing to collaborate with my old Ph.D. advisor on computational knot theory research.
